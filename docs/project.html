World Health Organization

The World Health Organization (WHO) is a specialized agency of the United Nations responsible for international public health. Established on April 7, 1948, its mission is to promote health, keep the world safe, and serve the vulnerable. Headquartered in Geneva, Switzerland, WHO works with 194 member states to address global health challenges, including disease prevention, health emergencies, and the promotion of universal health coverage. It plays a crucial role in coordinating responses to global health crises, setting standards for healthcare, and providing guidance on health-related policies and practices.

Runtime and improvements
Performance Insights:
  • Execution Time: Our queries and indexing took about 2 hours.
  • Time Dependency: The duration depends on the number of pages processed.
  • Optimization Suggestion:
    • Start the crawler directly from a specific region. For example Europe:
      • https://www.who.int/europe/home?v=welcome.
    • Time can be improved by optimizing the crawler, caching information, and running processes in parallel.

Hubs and Authorities

The returned pages contain Hubs. It can be seen that every page of an article about a disease, research, etc. has links to the disease or things related to the research, to related symptoms, etc. Therefore, the linked pages can be accessed from every page of an article about a disease. In addition, it can be seen that there are also authorities, which are the link pages of the articles. It seems that every article can be accessed through other disease or research pages.

PageRank

For each query, 20 relevant pages were returned. We tested the connectivity between these pages using an algorithm that goes through the links of each page and checks whether the other pages exist between them. We collected 10 links from the results, used the algorithm proposed in the course to create a graph and finally calculated the PageRank value.
